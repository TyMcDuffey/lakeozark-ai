How to automate your Lake vacation rental.

Running a short-term rental at the Lake can feel like a second full-time job that lives in your phone. The good news: most of what eats your day is repetitive, which means most of it can be automated. Done right, your guests will never know the difference, except that you answer faster.

By Ty McDuffey, J.D.  ·  June 2026  ·  7 min read

If you host on the Lake, you already know the rhythm of a busy summer. The bookings are great. The messages are relentless. And somewhere around the third "what's the WiFi password?" text at 10pm, you start wondering whether you own the rental or it owns you. This guide walks through what you can hand off to automation, where you still need to stay in the loop, and how to do it without turning your warm little lake house into a robot.

The part of hosting that actually eats your time

It is rarely the cleaning or the maintenance that wears hosts down. It is the communication. The same questions, over and over: check-in time, the gate code, the WiFi, the dock rules, where to rent a boat, where to buy groceries, what time is checkout. Multiply that by every booking across a full season and you are doing customer service at all hours instead of enjoying the Lake. That repetitive layer is exactly what automation is good at.

What you can automate, and should

Guest communication

Scheduled check-in and checkout messages, instant answers to your most common questions, automatic delivery of your house manual, and a friendly mid-stay check-in, all written in your voice and running across Airbnb, VRBO, and your direct bookings at the same time. This is the single biggest time-saver for most hosts.

Review responses

Prompt, professional replies to guest reviews, drafted in your tone, so your response rate and your listing's reputation stay strong without you composing each one by hand.

Owner and co-host reporting

If you manage units for other owners, automated summaries of bookings, revenue, and any issues save real hours and look far more professional than a hurried text once a month.

Turnover coordination

The moment a checkout happens, your cleaner and your handyman get an automatic notification with the details they need. No more being the human relay between every guest and every vendor.

Where a human still has to stay in the loop

Automation is not autopilot, and any host who treats it that way gets burned. A real person, you, should handle anything sensitive: a guest complaint, a damage situation, a refund decision, an unusual request. The right setup handles the routine eighty percent and routes the other twenty percent to you with full context, so your attention goes where it actually matters. Anything that could affect a guest's stay or your liability gets human eyes, every time.

Will it feel impersonal? Not if it is built right

This is the fear every good host has, and it is a fair one. Nobody wants their guests to feel like they are texting a vending machine. The trick is that good automation sounds like you. It uses your phrasing, your local recommendations, your warmth, and it is timely instead of obviously canned. Think about it from the guest's side: an instant, friendly, accurate answer at 9pm feels like great service, not bad service. Impersonal is a generic reply that ignores what they asked. Personal is the right answer, fast, in your voice, and that is exactly what a well-built system delivers.

Is automating messages allowed on Airbnb and VRBO?

Yes. Both platforms support scheduled and automated messaging, and plenty of hosts already rely on it. The only rule that matters is that you stay responsible for the content and step in whenever a situation needs a human. Using automation to respond faster is squarely allowed. Using it to ghost your guests is not.

A simple place to start

You do not have to automate everything at once, and you shouldn't. Start with the highest-volume, lowest-risk piece: your check-in sequence and the five questions you answer most often. Get those running cleanly and watch how many evenings you get back. Then layer in review responses, owner reporting, and turnover coordination one at a time. Within a single season, you will have clawed back a meaningful chunk of your life while your guests get faster, better service.
