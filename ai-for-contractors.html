The real problem

The leads aren't the problem. The missed calls are.

For a trades business, the phone is the lifeline, and a lot of it rings out. Industry data backs up what you already feel on a busy day: one widely cited study found that only about 38% of calls to small businesses are actually answered live, and research on home-services calls has found that roughly a quarter go unanswered, with each missed call potentially worth around $1,200 in lost work. At the Lake, where the season is short and customers are calling three contractors at once, the one who picks up wins.

You can't be on the roof and on the phone at the same time, and hiring a full-time receptionist for a seasonal swing rarely pencils out. That's the gap we close: a system that answers in your name, gets the job details, books it or texts the customer back instantly, and only hands you the calls worth stopping work for.

Questions trades ask

Straight answers, no tech-speak.

Will my customers know it's a robot?
It's built to sound natural and to be genuinely helpful, capturing the job details and getting the caller booked or texted back fast. You set the tone and exactly what it can promise. The alternative most callers face right now is voicemail, so a quick, useful answer is a step up either way.
What if I already have an answering service?
An answering service mostly takes a message and hands it back to you. Our system actually answers questions, qualifies the job, books it on your calendar, and texts the caller the second you miss a call, around the clock, usually for less than a per-call service costs.
Does it use my existing phone number?
In most setups, yes. We work with the number your customers already call so you don't have to reprint trucks, signs, or business cards. Mapping your current phone and scheduling setup is part of the free assessment.
Will it actually book jobs, or just take messages?
Book them. The point is to move a caller from "interested" to "on the calendar" while they're still on the line or in the text thread, and to flag the urgent ones straight to you.
I'm a one-person shop. Is this overkill?
It's the opposite. A one-person shop is exactly who loses the most to missed calls, because there's no one else to pick up. This gives you a front desk without the payroll.
What does it cost?
