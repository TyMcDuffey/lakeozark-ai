The real problem

Speed to lead decides who gets the commission.

Lake real estate is a contact sport. A buyer from St. Louis or Kansas City sees a waterfront listing at 9pm, fills out a form on three of them, and goes with whoever calls back first and sounds like they know the market. By the time you're out of your last showing, the lead has already booked with someone else. Meanwhile you're writing listing descriptions at the kitchen table, chasing past clients you meant to follow up with, and trying to keep a dozen deals straight.

AI fixes the part that's purely about speed and consistency. The instant a lead comes in, it gets a real, on-brand response and gets routed to you. Your listings get written in minutes instead of an hour. Your follow-up runs on its own so nobody slips through the cracks. You stay the agent, the relationship, and the local expert. The system just makes sure you're always the one who answered first.

Questions agents ask

Straight answers on AI and your business.

Will leads know they're talking to AI?
The system replies instantly in your voice to capture and qualify a new lead, then hands the live conversation to you. You decide exactly what it can say. The goal is simply that no inquiry sits unanswered while you're at a showing, which is where most leads are lost today.
Does it work with my CRM?
In most cases, yes. We build around the CRM and tools you already use, such as Follow Up Boss, kvCORE, or your brokerage's system, so leads, notes, and tasks land where you already work instead of in yet another app.
Who reviews the listing copy and messages?
You do. The system drafts descriptions, emails, and replies from your inputs, and you approve before anything is published or sent. That keeps everything accurate and within advertising and fair-housing rules, which is your responsibility as the agent.
I get most of my business by referral. Is this for me?
Yes, and it protects exactly that. Referrals you don't answer fast still go cold, and past clients you don't stay in touch with refer someone else. This keeps your sphere warm and makes sure no referral slips through while you're busy.
Will it sound generic?
It shouldn't, and that's the difference between a built system and an off-the-shelf bot. We train it on your voice and your market so the messages read like you, then tune it over time. Sounding local at the Lake is part of what wins the client.
What does it cost?
A one-time build fee plus an optional monthly fee to run it. Founding-client pricing currently starts at $2,500, with exact scope set in your free assessment. No hourly invoices.
