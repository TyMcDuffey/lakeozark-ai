Questions firms ask

Straight answers on AI and your bar license.

Is it even ethical for a lawyer to use AI?
Yes, when it's set up and supervised correctly. ABA Formal Opinion 512 confirms lawyers may use generative AI as long as they meet their existing duties: competence, confidentiality under Model Rule 1.6, supervision of the work, candor to the court, and reasonable fees. We configure and document your systems to those duties. Your state's rules control, and your attorneys remain responsible for every output.
Is AI safe for confidential client information?
It can be, with the right tools and configuration. We build on platforms that offer zero data retention, meaning your inputs aren't stored or used to train any model, and we put a written vendor agreement and firm AI policy in place before anything goes live. Consumer chatbots used casually are the real risk; a properly configured system is the fix.
What is ABA Formal Opinion 512?
Issued July 29, 2024, it's the American Bar Association's first formal ethics opinion on generative AI. It walks through confidentiality, competence, supervision, communicating with clients about AI use, billing, and vetting AI vendors. We use it, alongside your state's Rules of Professional Conduct, as the framework for every law-firm build.
Will AI invent fake cases like in the news?
That happens when lawyers ask a general chatbot to write a brief and file it unchecked. Our drafting workflows are grounded in your own documents and include verification steps, and a lawyer reviews everything before it leaves the firm. We design specifically against that failure mode.
Do you give legal or ethics advice?
No. We're an AI implementation vendor. We don't advise you on your specific ethical obligations or act as your firm's counsel. The founder's J.D. informs how we build and document systems so your attorneys can satisfy the duties they already have.
We're a small or solo firm. Is this overkill?
The opposite. Small and solo firms get the most leverage, because you don't have associates to absorb the repetitive work. Founding-client pricing starts at $2,500 for a compliant setup, and we scope exactly what's worth doing in a free assessment.
